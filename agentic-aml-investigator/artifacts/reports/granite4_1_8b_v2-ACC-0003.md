## Case Summary
Account **ACC-0003** associated with the business "Mcclain, Miller and Henderson" in the United States exhibited suspicious activity involving structured cash deposits. The account received 25 cash deposits deliberately kept within the $8,500–$10,000 band between March 4, 2026, and May 30, 2026, totaling **$230,077**. This pattern aligns with structuring behavior aimed at avoiding the $10,000 Currency Transaction Report (CTR) threshold.

## Evidence
- **[EV-01]**: Structuring scan indicates 25 cash deposits in the $8,500-$10,000 band totalling **$230,077** between 2026-03-04 and 2026-05-30 (maximum 2 per day). Additionally, there are 59 deposits of $10,000 or more totaling **$721,138**.
- **[EV-02]**: Profile data shows the account opened on July 9, 2024, with total inflows of **$2,764,249** and outflows of **$523,311**. It has interacted with **253 counterparties** across **5 countries**, being active for **90 days**. The top flow types are cash deposits (**180 instances totaling $1,422,161**) and transfers (**218 instances totaling $1,342,088**), with wire outflows amounting to **$523,311**.
- **[EV-03]**: Sanctions screening of the 253 counterparties against 19,065 OFAC SDN entries (fuzzy cutoff 87) found **no matches**.

## Risk Assessment
The risk assessment assigns a score of **75**, categorizing the activity as **structuring**. Key factors include:
1. The account received **25 cash deposits** within the $8,500–$10,000 band, totaling **$230,077** between March 4 and May 30, 2026 ([EV-01]).
2. These deposits are indicative of a structuring pattern designed to evade the $10,000 CTR threshold ([EV-01]).
3. The account's typical behavior involves frequent cash transactions (**180 deposits totaling $1,422,161**) over **90 active days** with numerous counterparties across multiple countries ([EV-02]).

No additional evidence is required, and the assessment recommends **escalation** of this case.
